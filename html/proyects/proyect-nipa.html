A Dungeon Crawler with puzzles in which you can't shoot.

Download Nipa on Steam
Nipa is a Dungeon Crawler with puzzles where you can't shoot. You control Ney, a being from a pacifist race who must climb the White Tower to redeem himself and save his people.

Information

  • Engine: Unreal Engine 5.2.1
  • Language: C++
  • Status: Completed
  • Development: Oct 2023 - Sep 2024
  • Genre: Dungeon Crawler
  • Platforms:
    • Windows (Steam)
    • MacOS (Steam)

Summary

  • Nipa was our final project for the Master's Degree in Video Game Development at the Complutense University of Madrid in the 2023-24 academic year.
  • I worked on Nipa as part of the programming team under the mentorship of Gonzalo Florez (MercurySteam).
  • After submitting it, all my team was congratulated by the faculty of the course. My final grade was 10/10.
  • Nipa was also recognized by different videogame events, receiving the 3rd best game award at the Guerrilla Game Festival in its 2024 edition and being nominated for best game of the year by PlayStation Talents.

Contributions

The whole programming team was heavily involved in the development of the game, and I was able to touch all the systems at some point. However, my main work was in:

  • Gameplay systems:
    • Game camera: Component that made the camera follow the character with dynamic parameters.
    • Respawn system:
      • Checkpoint and respawn system: When passing through certain triggers, the player saved the position to teleport to this point in case of taking damage.
      • Level restart system: When returning to the last saved checkpoint, the level had to be reset to avoid unfair deaths or soft blocks.
  • User interface:
    • Gameplay HUD: Energy bar displayed in the upper left corner. It shows the amount of available energy the player has, changes color depending on the skill being used and has animations to make it more dynamic and visual.
    • Ability unlock HUD: Ability unlock animation, making it both dynamic and smooth and comfortable to the eye.
    • Dash ability HUD.
    • Death Transition: Transition implemented to allow scene restart without showing object destruction or camera cuts.
  • Menus: All the menus of the game were implemented by me, allowing its use with both controller and mouse, even combining both.
    • I also programmed the game options system, allowing the choice of vertical sync, window size and more customizations.
  • VFX:
    • Player's trail.
    • Ability 'Mind extension'.
    • Ability 'Dash': 'Dash' trail, leaving copies of the player behind with his same animation pose.
    • Ability 'Incorporeal'.
    • Ability 'Time slowdown'.
    • 'Darkness enemy': Mass of darkness that had to cover part of the screen without being too big in order not to slow down the game.
  • Technical art: Organized working pipeline with the art department to introduce part of their assets in the game, as well as to adapt the ones that gave problems.
  • Debug systems: Implementation of systems to facilitate the debugging of the game.
