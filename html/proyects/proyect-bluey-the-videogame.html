Summary

  • I worked on Bluey: The Videogame at Artax Games as a Junior Generalist Programmer.
  • It is inspired by the well-known TV series Bluey, created by Ludo Studio and distributed by BBC Studio.
  • My stage in Artax Games was developed since April 2024, when I started to work combining it with the last months of the Master of Videogame Development that I was studying at the Complutense University of Madrid. Once I finished my studies I continued working in the studio until early 2025.
  • My work began with the refactoring of all the game systems, as the amount of bugs and problems was considerable. This meant that I had to adapt quickly to a very advanced project, and although it had its difficulties, after a while I was used to its structure and was ready to implement new features.
  • Developed by a small team in which I had to work both under the direction of a boss and on my own.
  • Bluey: The Videogame was a success at launch, selling more than 2M units during its first year.

Contributions

As a Junior Generalist Programmer I worked with all the game systems. However, my main work was in:

  • Refactorings:
    • State machine implementations to interacting elements.
    • Debugging multiple bugs in any system.
  • Gameplay systems:
    • Implementation of gameplay modules.
    • Re-skin system: Implementation of a system that transformed a “base” player into the character he/she decided to play with.
    • Cosmetic system: Improvement of the system in the characters.
    • Camera system:
      • Handling of different types of cameras, each one with its own parameters: motion interpolation, rails, limits, etc.
    • Cinematic system:
      • Implementation of episodic cinematics within the game, which alternated them with gameplay parts.
      • Use of Unity timelines for the manipulation of objects during them.
    • Management of the save system both locally and in the cloud (PC and consoles).
  • Local multiplayer system:
    • Handling of inputs and reconnections.
    • Implementation of menus for player reconnection.
    • Implementation of interactions with up to four players.
  • User interface and menus:
    • Creation of several menus: selection of characters, activities, cosmetics, collectibles, etc.
    • Canvas handling for all types of HUD and on-screen prompts.
  • AI systems:
    • Implementation of behaviors in NPCs both by Behaviour Trees and solely by code.
    • Path calculation in navmesh full of obstacles.
  • Scripting resources:
    • Math Utils: Implementation of various mathematical scripts, such as 'Calculation of the nearest point', 'Number of characters in a given area', etc.
    • Use of Third Party APIs: Implementation of APIs such as DOTween for the facilitation of certain actions and animations.
  • Animations:
    • Implemented both in-game and cinematic animations created with Spine 2D.
    • Creation of simple animations both by code and using Unity's timeline.
  • SFX implementation:
    • Both one-shot and loops using FMOD.
    • Creation of buses for diversification of situations.
  • VFX:
    • Simple shaders.
    • Simple particle systems.
  • Offline build: Familiarization of the build creation pipeline, with special emphasis on the SDKs needed for PlayStation, Xbox Series and Nintendo Switch.
    • At certain points in the development I had to make builds for both Windows (Steam) and all platforms where the game was released.
