Summary

  • Vertical Slide was an extra work we did for the Master's Degree in Video Game Development at the Complutense University of Madrid in the 2023-24 course.
  • On this occasion I decided to take part of the design team, because that's where it was most needed. I also saw it as a challenge to create an adventure with some role-playing components, and given my experience and love for RPGs, my favorite genre, I did not hesitate. It was also interesting to design a responsive interface for the full range of mobile devices where the game could be played.
  • The idea for the game evolved from 'Don't make me laugh', the project with which we participated in the Global Game Jam 2024. As our game won honorable mention for best art and second prize for best game by the public at the headquarters of the Complutense University of Madrid, we were encouraged to continue changing some concepts. Despite being an extra work of the master, the whole team was tutored by Gonzalo Florez (MercurySteam) and Francisco José de la Torre Chamorro (aka. Xito) (Digital Legends).
  • The final grade of the project was 10/10.
  • After finishing it, we polished some more details to take it to fairs like Guerrilla Game Festival 2024, but finally we decided to finish it to give way to other projects.

Contributions

As I explained in the summary, in this project I was part of the Design team, but more specifically my main work was:

  • Combat Design:
    • Given my extensive experience in the RPG genre (Pokémon, Final Fantasy, Mother, Sea of Stars, etc.) I decided to create my own combat system. This had to be simple given that the 'target' of the game was casual gamers, especially since the platform is a mobile device.
    • The result was a card-paper-scissors-rock, largely inspired by the gameplay of Buckshot Roulette. It consists of each card of a color (green, red or blue) representing an element that is part of a triangle of strengths and weaknesses, beating one but losing to the other, just like in the popular children's game.
      • The component that they are cards and both the player and the AI have a predefined number at the beginning of the combat is the necessary point to limit the randomness and prioritize a strategy that, although simple, challenging.
      • At the beginning of the combat, the number of cards of each element of the enemy is shown for a certain time, depending on the difficulty, and the player has to remember them during the combat to make his 'probability calculations' of which is the safest card that the enemy will use in his next turn.
    • We considered adding 'passives' to certain cards to add more depth to the combat, but from Programming the scope was limited and finally it could not be done.
  • Interface design: All of them responsive to adapt to the mobile device where the game is played. All of them can be seen in the Gallery.
    • Story interface design.
    • Combat interface design: Due to the numerous iterations that the combat system suffered, this one was also changing throughout the development.
  • Art:
    • Part of the game's final assets were created by me.
  • Marketing:
    • The game has a trailer that can be seen at the top of the page. This was designed and edited by me, and served as marketing to take the game later to the Guerrilla Game Festival 2024.
